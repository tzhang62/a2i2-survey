Introduction

We invite you to take part in a research study. Please take as much time as you need to read the consent form. You may want to discuss it with your family, friends, or your personal doctor. If you find any of the language difficult to understand, please ask questions. If you decide to participate, you will be asked to sign this form. A copy of the signed form will be given to you for your records.

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to understand how artificial intelligence systems can simulate individuals' decision-making and communication in stressful emergency situations like fire rescues. We hope to learn how individual factors, such as personality traits, moral values, and personal circumstances, affect how people respond to emergency instructions and persuasion strategies. You are invited to participate in this research because you meet the eligibility requirements for participation, which include being 18 years or older, a fluent English speaker, and having normal or corrected-to-normal (20/20) vision. About one hundred participants will take part in the study. This research is being funded by the United States Air Force.

Procedures

If you decide to take part, this is what will happen: you will first be asked to provide your consent by clicking "I consent and continue." After giving consent, you will complete an online questionnaire about your background, personality, values, moral beliefs, and any potential needs or constraints relevant to a fire-rescue situation. You will then engage in a simulated emergency conversation in which you will interact via text with a virtual emergency operator powered by artificial intelligence; during this interaction, you will take on the role of the person being rescued. You will share your preference, willingness to follow the instructions and how natural the conversation felt. The entire procedure should take 15 to 20 minutes. After the completion of the survey, we will request your email address solely to send you compensation. To ensure data quality and anonymity, we'll compare your responses with known patterns of effort. If the responses do not meet our data quality standards, compensation will not be offered.

Risk and Discomforts

Participation in this study involves minimal risk. Some participants may experience mild psychological discomfort or stress when engaging with the simulated fire-rescue scenario, as it depicts an emergency. You may also experience minor fatigue while completing the online questionnaire or interacting with the virtual operator. Although your responses will be recorded anonymously, there is always a small risk of loss of confidentiality, as is possible with any online data collection. No physical risks, medical procedures, or in-person activities involved.

Surveys/Questionnaires/Interviews

Some of the questions may make you feel uneasy or embarrassed. You can choose to skip or stop answering any questions you don't want to.

Breach of Confidentiality

There is a small risk that people who are not connected with this study will learn your identity or your personal information.

Benefits

There are no direct benefits to you from taking part in this study. However, your participation in this study may help us better understand communication and decision-making during emergency situations, which could contribute to improved emergency response systems

Privacy/Confidentiality

We will keep your records for this study confidential as far as permitted by law. However, if we are required to do so by law, we will disclose confidential information about you. Efforts will be made to limit the use and disclosure of your personal information, including research study and medical records, to people who are required to review this information. We may publish the information from this study in journals or present it at meetings. If we do, we will not use your name.

The University of Southern California's Institutional Review Board (IRB) and Human Research Protection Program (HRPP) may review your records. Organizations that may also inspect and copy your information include the research team responsible for conducting this study. No outside agencies, sponsors, or federal regulators will have access to your identifiable information.

Officials of the U.S. Army Research Protections Office and the Army Research Laboratory's Human Research Protection Program are permitted by law to inspect the records obtained in this study to ensure compliance with laws and regulations for experiments using human subjects.

Possible Future use of data

Your data will be maintained confidentially and may be shared with other researchers. The research may be about similar or unrelated topics to this study. Our goal is to make more research possible. We plan to keep your data indefinitely. If shared, data will be transferred securely. If you are not comfortable with this, you should not participate in this study.

Alternatives

An alternative would be not to participate in this study.

Payments/Compensation

You will receive a $5 Amazon gift card for taking part in this study. Compensation will be provided after you complete the study session. Payments for research participation are considered taxable income; however, because the amount in this study is below the IRS reporting threshold, you will not receive a tax form from the University.

Voluntary Participation

It is your choice whether to participate. If you choose to participate, you may change your mind and leave the study at any time. If you decide not to participate, or choose to end your participation in this study, you will not be penalized or lose any benefits that you are otherwise entitled to.
